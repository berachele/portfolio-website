Intro

Welcome to my portfolio! I've recently started Lambda School's Full Stack Web Development program. I started February of 2020 and have already learned a great deal. So far, I've learned to use HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Later in the program, I will continue to learn other coding languages such as Node, React, Python, Redux and SQL. this has been an incredible journey thus far and am excited to see where it takes me.

Make sure to checkout the projects link to see what I'm currently working on!

Sweet Eats Bakery

Sweet Eats Bakery was one of my first projects while starting Lambda. I made the content of the HTML more semantically pleasing for easy navigation. In HTML, I also added links, images, and created class selectors for my CSS code. In CSS followed the instructions to style the webpage. I dug deeper by importing different fonts, and styling the background, images, and sections a bit differently to test my knowledge on CSS.

CSS+HTML Practice

Digging my toes into CSS and HTML more, I needed to exactly duplicate my styling to match the webpage that I was given. I'm a perfectionist, so this was a fun challenge for me! Definitely got to explore how the inspector tool worked for this project.

Interested in seeing more? Check out my code here!

Great Idea Webpage

Starting my project for the Great Idea Webspage was a little bit of a struggle for me. We first learned how to use display and inline-blocking styles in our CSS. This was before learning flexbox--I am so grateful we struggled through using "display:inline-block;" because I appreciate flexbox that much more now! We also started using git in our terminal to locally work on our project before pushing it publicly through GitHub.

Interested in seeing more? Check out my code here!

Sprint Challenge

Sprint Challenges at Lambda are timed tests to see if you've mastered what you've learned earlier that week. In this Challenge, we had three hours to label our HTML semantically, and style our webpage to exactly match the mock up that was given to us. Using two different HTML pages and styling on the same CSS. With time to spare from the Challenge, I was able to stretch my abilities more by HTML and CSS styling the Contact page to match the rest of the webpage. It wasn't completed, but I was proud for what I was able to accomplish!

About Rachele

Talent is a pursued interest. Anything that you're willing to practice, you can do.
-Bob Ross

Rachele grew up in a variety of cities in Utah, including South Jordan, Lehi, Logan and Orem. She graduated Utah Valley University with her Associates of Science Degree in Spring of 2018. Shortly before graduating, Rachele married her sweetheart April of 2018. Before joining the Lambda School program, Rachele previously worked at a medical billing office for almost four years.

How did Rachele go from medical coding to coding web languages? Her brother introduced Rachele to Lambda School when they teamed up with Jessica Livingston for the 2019 Summer Hack's Program. At that time of her life, she wasn't able to qualify for the program--but it introduced it to her. Over the next couple months in her spare time, she continued learning the basics of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. And although some parts of it were challenging, she knew this is what she wanted to do with her life and Lambda was going to get her there.

Rachele would like to thank her family who conitnually loves and supports her through her life goals. She would like to specially give thanks to her brother--for encouraging her to learn Web Development and coaching her through coding--and her husband, who made every sacrifice possible to make her goals a reality.
